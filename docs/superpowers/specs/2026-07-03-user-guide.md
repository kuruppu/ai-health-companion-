## Getting Started Guide

### Your First Conversation with AI

After onboarding, you're ready to start! Here's how to have your first conversation.

#### Opening the Chat

1. Tap the chat icon on the bottom navigation
2. You'll see a friendly greeting from your AI coach
3. The AI might ask: "Hey! What are you up to? Planning a meal?"

#### Example First Conversation

**You:** "I made chicken curry for dinner, how much should I eat?"

**AI:** "Great choice! Chicken curry can be really healthy. Let me help you out. What did you put in it?"

**You:** "Chicken breast, onions, tomatoes, coconut milk, and spices. Eating with rice."

**AI:** "Perfect! Here's what I recommend for your dinner tonight:
- 1 cup of chicken curry (about 300g)
- 1/2 cup of rice (about 125g cooked)
- Add a side of vegetables if you have them

This comes to about 450 calories, which fits perfectly in your remaining calorie budget for today (you have 600 calories left). You'll still have room for a small snack later if you're hungry.

Does this portion sound good to you?"

**You:** "Yes, that works!"

**AI:** "Awesome! Enjoy your dinner. Let me know later how it was, and if you felt satisfied. This helps me learn what works best for you."

### Understanding AI Responses

Your AI coach doesn't just throw numbers at you. Here's what to expect:

**What the AI provides:**
- **Visual portion sizes:** "1 cup", "palm-sized piece", "handful"
- **Calorie context:** How it fits in your daily budget
- **Flexibility:** Adjusts based on what you've eaten already today
- **Personalization:** Learns if you prefer larger lunches or dinners

**What the AI doesn't do:**
- Make you count every calorie
- Restrict entire food groups
- Judge your choices
- Force you to eat foods you don't like

---

### How the AI Learns Your Preferences

The more you chat with your AI coach, the smarter it gets about YOUR preferences.

**The AI learns:**
- Your favorite foods and meals
- Portion sizes that satisfy you
- Times you usually eat meals
- Foods that keep you full longer
- Your workout preferences and fitness level
- How you respond to different motivation styles

**Example:** After two weeks, instead of asking "What's in your curry?", the AI might say "Making your usual chicken curry? Want the portion size that worked well last time, or do you need more today?"

#### Understanding Calorie Recommendations

You don't need to obsess over numbers, but here's what's happening behind the scenes:

**Your daily calorie target:**
- Calculated based on your age, height, weight, activity level
- Adjusted for safe weight loss (usually 500-750 calorie deficit)
- Example: 1,500 calories per day for sustainable weight loss

**How it's divided:**
- Breakfast: ~400 calories
- Lunch: ~450 calories
- Dinner: ~500 calories
- Snacks: ~150 calories

**But here's the key:** The AI is flexible. If you have a bigger lunch, it'll adjust your dinner recommendation. You don't have to think about this - the AI handles it.

---

#### Flex Meals: What They Are & How to Use Them

Flex meals are your "no-guilt" meals where you can enjoy your favorites without derailing your progress.

**What is a flex meal?**
- 1-2 meals per week where you eat what you love
- Birthday dinners, date nights, Sunday brunch, etc.
- You don't have to count calories or measure portions

**How to use them:**
1. **Plan ahead:** Tell your AI "I have dinner plans Friday night, marking it as a flex meal"
2. **Be spontaneous:** "Office lunch invitation, using a flex meal"
3. **Enjoy guilt-free:** The AI adjusts your week to accommodate it

**AI's role:**
- Tracks your flex meals (so you don't overdo it)
- Adjusts other days slightly to keep you on track
- Reminds you that you have one available: "You have 1 flex meal left this week - saving it for the weekend?"

**Important:** Flex meals aren't binge meals. Eat until satisfied, not until uncomfortable. The AI will check in afterward: "How was dinner? Did you enjoy it?"

---

#### Logging What You Actually Ate

After your meal, the AI will follow up to see how it went.

**Follow-up questions:**
- "Did you eat the portions I suggested?"
- "How full do you feel? (1-10 scale)"
- "Did anything stop you from eating what we planned?"

**Why this matters:**
- Helps the AI learn your true hunger levels
- Adjusts future recommendations
- Identifies patterns (do you always overeat at dinner? Are breakfast portions too small?)

**Be honest!** The AI doesn't judge. If you ate more, just say so. The AI will adjust and help you stay on track.

**Example exchange:**

**AI:** "How was that chicken curry dinner?"

**You:** "I ate more rice than you suggested, maybe a full cup instead of half"

**AI:** "No worries! That's about 100 extra calories. Still well within a healthy range. Were you extra hungry today, or did the half cup just not feel like enough?"

**You:** "Didn't feel like enough"

**AI:** "Got it! I'll remember you prefer more rice with curry. Next time I'll suggest 3/4 cup instead of 1/2 cup. We'll make this work for YOU."

## Tips & Best Practices

### How to Get the Most Out of Your AI Coach

**1. Be consistent with logging**
- Log every meal, even if it wasn't perfect
- The AI learns from your patterns
- Honesty = better recommendations

**2. Communicate openly**
- Having a rough day? Tell your AI
- Extra hungry? Let it know
- Cravings? The AI can help strategize

**3. Log immediately after eating**
- Don't wait until end of day (you'll forget details)
- Quick 30-second chat right after meals
- Keeps calorie tracking accurate

**4. Use voice input when busy**
- Hands full with baby? Use voice
- Faster than typing
- Just as accurate

**5. Review your weekly summary**
- Every Sunday, the AI shows your week
- See patterns (did you overeat on weekends?)
- Celebrate wins
- Adjust for next week

**Before meals:**
- 10-15 minutes before eating
- Ask for portion guidance
- Make better decisions when you're not starving

- Right after finishing

### How to Handle Cravings

**Step 1: Tell your AI immediately**

**You:** "I'm craving pizza so bad right now"

**AI:** "I hear you! Let's figure this out together. Quick questions:
1. When did you last eat?
2. Did you drink water recently?
3. Are you actually hungry, or is it stress/boredom?"

**Strategy 1 - Have a small portion:**
- "Order one slice of your favorite pizza"

- "Use a flex meal if you want more"

- "Make a healthier version at home"

- "Drink a glass of water"
- "Wait 15 minutes"
- "Still want it? Then have it mindfully"

**Strategy 4 - Schedule it:**
- "Add it to your flex meal this weekend"
- "Something to look forward to"
- "Easier to resist when you know it's coming"

## FAQ (Frequently Asked Questions)

### "What if I don't have internet?"

**Limited offline functionality:**
- You can view your dashboard and previous conversations
- You cannot chat with AI or get new recommendations
- Reminders still work (they're scheduled locally)

**When you reconnect:**
- All your offline data syncs automatically
- The AI reviews what you logged offline
- Provides catch-up recommendations

**Pro tip:** Download workout videos when you have WiFi so you can do them offline.

---

### "How accurate are the calorie calculations?"

**AI uses multiple data sources:**
- USDA food database (most comprehensive)
- Restaurant nutrition data
- Recipe analysis (based on ingredients)
- User feedback (over time, learns portion accuracy)

**Accuracy level:**
- Most foods: ±50 calories
- Restaurant meals: ±100 calories (estimated)
- Home-cooked meals: ±30 calories (if you provide full ingredient list)

**The truth:** Perfect accuracy isn't needed. Consistency is what matters. If the AI is consistently estimating your meals the same way, you'll still lose weight at the predicted rate.

**The AI is transparent:**
- "This is an estimate based on typical chicken curry recipes"
- "Restaurant portions vary - this is approximate"
- Your weight loss results validate the accuracy over time

---

### "Can I eat foods not in the database?"

**Absolutely!** Here's how:

**Option 1: Describe it to the AI**

**You:** "I ate a homemade sweet my mom made - it has rice flour, jaggery, coconut, and ghee"

**AI:** "I don't have that exact recipe, but based on the ingredients, here's my estimate: about 150 calories per piece. How many pieces did you have? I'll log it and we'll adjust if needed based on how you feel later."

**Option 2: Compare to similar foods**

**You:** "I had a local street food called pani puri"

**AI:** "I'll estimate based on similar fried snacks with filling. About 10 puris would be approximately 300 calories. Sound about right?"

**The AI learns:** Next time you eat that food, the AI remembers and gets more accurate.

---

### "What if I miss a workout?"

**No punishment, only support:**

**AI:** "I notice you didn't do yesterday's workout. That's totally okay! What happened?
- Too tired?
- Didn't have time?
- Wasn't feeling it?

Your answer helps me suggest better workouts for your real life."

**The plan adjusts:**
- The AI doesn't "punish" with extra workouts

**Step 5: Reinstall the app**
- Delete app (your data is safe in the cloud)
- Reinstall from app store
- Log back in
